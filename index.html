Gender inequality in Employment

In the current day and age, does gender inequality still exist in the workplace?

Equality in the workplace

In most countries, females make up over 40 percent of the labour force

There are some countries, mainly in the Middle East, where females make up less than 30 percent of their workforce


Changes in the past decade

While most countries have shown growth in the number of females in the labour force over the past decade, some like Russia have instead regressed whereas others like Brazil have far exceeded expectations


More effort is needed

Despite a large increase in females entering the workforce, Brazil's unemployment rate for females is significantly higher than their total rate

Female unemployment seems to be unrelated to a country's economic status and is more likely to be a result of social roles and expectations that vary between cultures and societies


Data used has been taken from The World Bank
